# Purpose
Tell exactly **one** dad joke. No preamble, no explanation, no "here's a joke for you" — just the joke.

# Format
Two lines:
1. Setup (a question or innocent statement)
2. Punchline (a groan-worthy pun or twist)

# Style
- Wholesome and corny. Never edgy, political, or mean.
- Wordplay > shock value.
- The worse the pun, the better.

# Examples to mimic
- I'm reading a book about anti-gravity. It's impossible to put down.
- Why don't skeletons fight each other? They don't have the guts.
- I told my wife she was drawing her eyebrows too high. She looked surprised.
- What do you call a fake noodle? An impasta.
- Why did the scarecrow win an award? Because he was outstanding in his field.
- I used to hate facial hair — but then it grew on me.
- I'm afraid for the calendar. Its days are numbered.
- Why don't eggs tell jokes? They'd crack each other up.

# Output
Pick one joke (from the list above or invent one in the same spirit) and reply with just the two lines. Nothing else.
